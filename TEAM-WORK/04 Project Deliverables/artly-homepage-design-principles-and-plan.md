## Strategic Summary

The homepage should introduce Artly as a specialized physical AI company that turns real-world human expertise, deployment data, proprietary hardware, and learning models into commercially deployable robots. The first screen needs to explain the category quickly, establish credibility, and give different visitor types a clear route forward.

The story should move from category leadership to practical deployment:

1. Artly powers intelligent robots through commercialized robot training infrastructure.
2. The platform makes robot training more accessible and practical for nontechnical operators.
3. Barista Bot is the concrete proof point: a specialized robot already designed for challenging commercial environments.
4. Deployments improve the platform over time, creating a compounding data and intelligence advantage.

## Design Principles

| Principle | Planning Guidance |
| --- | --- |
| Lead with category clarity | Use plain language around physical AI and robot training so first-time visitors understand Artly within seconds. |
| Make the product real | Pair platform claims with demonstration media, Barista Bot details, real logos, resource tiles, and press proof. |
| Serve multiple visitor paths | Operators need demos and capability details. Investors need scale and intelligence-layer strategy. Press and consumers need simple orientation and resources. |
| Keep AI claims grounded | Emphasize training, expert input, real-world feedback loops, proprietary hardware, and commercial deployments instead of vague AI language. |
| Show compounding advantage | Repeat the idea that each deployment strengthens the system, especially near the closing catch-all section. |
| Use CTAs by intent | Reserve high-commitment CTAs for demos, expert conversations, investment interest, event signup, or lead capture. Use "Learn More" for early-stage visitors. |

## Risk / Quality Notes

| Risk | Why It Matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Overstated AI or robotics claims | First-time visitors, press, and investors will scrutinize credibility. | Tie claims to training process, deployment feedback, hardware, and proof assets. |
| Numeric claims without substantiation | Claims like "30-Minute Training" and "1.2M+ Actions" may require evidence or context. | Confirm source, measurement definition, and approval before publishing. |
| Investment language compliance | Investment-facing copy may create legal or regulatory exposure. | Route final investment copy through the appropriate approval path. |
| Logo permission gaps | Public use of logos can require partner/customer approval. | Use only approved logos and maintain source records. |
| Vague physical AI positioning | The category may be unfamiliar to early-stage visitors. | Define the platform in concrete operational language before using abstract terms. |
| Video accessibility | Demonstration video is central to comprehension. | Include captions/transcript, strong poster image, and accessible controls. |
