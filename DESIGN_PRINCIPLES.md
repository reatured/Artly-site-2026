# Artly Design Principles

## Core Style Direction

Artly's design style is **Precision Hospitality**: advanced robotics made warm, useful, and commercially credible.

The site should feel like a real service business powered by robotics, not like a generic AI startup, a consumer gadget page, or a dark sci-fi robotics brand.

## Primary References

Use these references as the main design anchors:

- **Agility Robotics**: real operational proof, clear deployment language, clean commercial confidence.
- **Physical**: disciplined section sequencing, spacious layouts, focused narrative.
- **Intrinsic**: plain-language explanations of complex robotics.

Use **Figure** and **Boston Dynamics** only as light inspiration for robot-first media impact. Do not copy their cinematic or heroic tone.

## Visual Direction

Use a mostly light, clean interface with selective dark media sections. The palette should combine warm hospitality neutrals, coffee tones, stainless steel gray, soft black, and one precise technology accent such as signal blue or electric teal.

Avoid purple AI gradients, beige-heavy lifestyle design, generic SaaS blue, abstract blobs, and overly futuristic visuals.

The first impression should show the Artly robot in a real service context. Real robot media, coffee preparation, customer interaction, and deployment environments should carry the design. Avoid abstract AI illustrations unless they explain a specific system behavior.

## Typography

Use a modern, premium sans-serif. Headlines should be large, clear, and product-first. Avoid futuristic fonts.

Use short technical labels sparingly for system details.

## Homepage Structure

1. **Hero**
   - Full-width real robot or coffee-service media.
   - Concrete headline, not abstract mission language.
   - Suggested tone: "Robotic coffee, built for real service."
   - Primary CTA: "Book a Demo"
   - Secondary CTA: "Explore Artly"

2. **Proof**
   - Show locations, partners, press, deployment facts, or operational credibility.
   - Every major claim should have proof nearby.

3. **How Artly Works**
   - Explain the system in three or four simple steps:
     - Configure the experience.
     - Deploy the robot.
     - Serve consistent drinks.
     - Monitor and support operations.

4. **The Artly System**
   - Present hardware, AI, coffee craft, and service operations as one integrated system.
   - Use split layouts with robot media and concise feature modules.

5. **Commercial Use Cases**
   - Include relevant spaces such as corporate campuses, hospitality, retail, events, universities, and transit.
   - Copy should be practical and buyer-oriented.

6. **Coffee Experience**
   - Make the experience feel warm and human.
   - Show drink quality, ordering, pickup, interaction, and consistency.

7. **Deployment and Support**
   - Explain installation, maintenance, uptime, remote monitoring, training, and scaling.
   - This section should reduce buyer anxiety.

8. **Shopify Commerce / Inquiry Flow**
   - Use clear product/service cards, demo request CTAs, solution pages, and contact flows.
   - Keep Shopify interactions familiar and easy.

## Motion

Use restrained, premium motion only:

- Slow hero video.
- Gentle scroll reveals.
- Subtle hover states.
- Simple system-flow animations.
- Reduced-motion support.

## Avoid

- Unsupported "proven" or "revolutionary" claims.
- Generic AI buzzwords.
- Overly dark sci-fi design.
- Developer-only language.
- Consumer home robot positioning.
- Excessive cards.
- Decorative orbs, blobs, and gradients.
- Copy that explains features without showing proof.

## Five-Second Test

Within five seconds, a first-time visitor should understand:

**Artly is a real robotic coffee system for commercial spaces, and it can be deployed, supported, and trusted.**
