# Artly Design Principles

## Core Style Direction

Artly's design style is **Precision Hospitality**: advanced robotics made warm, useful, and commercially credible. The more specific visual target is a warm, minimal, gallery-like product website for a robotic coffee system.

The site should feel like a premium robotics product showroom with specialty-coffee warmth: minimal UI, clean spacing, soft natural light, architectural surfaces, and product-first storytelling. It should not feel like a generic AI startup, a rustic coffee shop, a consumer gadget page, a SaaS dashboard, or a dark sci-fi robotics brand.

## Primary References

Use these references as the main design anchors:

- **Agility Robotics**: real operational proof, clear deployment language, clean commercial confidence.
- **Physical**: disciplined section sequencing, spacious layouts, focused narrative.
- **Intrinsic**: plain-language explanations of complex robotics.

Use **Figure** and **Boston Dynamics** only as light inspiration for robot-first media impact. Do not copy their cinematic or heroic tone.

Use the internal robotic coffee showroom reference as the main art-direction anchor for homepage composition: floating pill navigation, full-width product render or real robot media, warm sunlight, thin dividers, gallery spacing, large product title bands, image-led cards, and small light-blue accents.

## Visual Direction

Use a mostly light, clean interface with selective dark media sections. The palette should combine warm off-white, cream, light concrete, stainless steel gray, soft black, coffee tones, and one precise light-blue technology accent.

Avoid purple AI gradients, generic SaaS blue, abstract blobs, overly futuristic visuals, heavy rustic coffee textures, dark wood, busy graphics, noisy backgrounds, oversized colorful buttons, and one-note beige styling.

The first impression should show the Artly robot or Barista Bot product in a real or highly believable service context. Real robot media, approved product renders, coffee preparation, customer interaction, and deployment environments should carry the design. Avoid abstract AI illustrations unless they explain a specific system behavior.

Art direction keywords:

- Minimal.
- Premium.
- Warm industrial.
- Robotic coffee.
- Soft sunlight.
- Gallery-like.
- Product-first.
- Clean grid.
- Cream, light concrete, steel, black, and light blue.

## Layout Direction

Use an Apple-like product landing page structure, but keep the tone grounded in commercial robotics and service operations.

- Hero: full-width robot/product media, architectural wall/floor context, warm natural light, soft shadows, and spacious composition.
- Hero proof line: a concise operational metric can sit over or below the hero, with the number highlighted in light blue. Only use exact metrics when source-approved.
- Product title band: large product name on the left, short marketing statement and small CTA on the right, separated by thin divider lines.
- Content sections: centered titles followed by image-led cards or clean split layouts.
- Cards: rounded image cards with generous spacing, minimal decoration, and no heavy shadows.
- Dividers: thin gray lines for structure between major sections.

## Typography

Use Work Sans for body copy, UI text, forms, navigation, and dense operational content. Use Space Grotesk for headings, display moments, and accent typography. Headlines should be large, clear, and product-first. Avoid futuristic fonts.

Use short technical labels sparingly for system details. Large display titles can be uppercase and elegant, but keep letter spacing normal. Do not introduce Inter, Helvetica Neue, Neue Haas Grotesk, Suisse Intl, or other typefaces unless Richard explicitly changes the font system.

Suggested type roles:

- Display title: large uppercase Space Grotesk, regular to bold weight depending on context.
- H1/H2: Space Grotesk, direct product-first phrasing.
- Body/UI/nav/forms: Work Sans.
- CTA labels: Work Sans, small, clear, and action-specific.

## Header Chrome

Use a soft-metal or translucent glass header shell with a compact logo capsule, rounded pill navigation, and a restrained right-side utility/action capsule. Header navigation and utility labels use Work Sans. Artly brand/display moments use Space Grotesk.

Remove starter-store language such as `Welcome to our store`. Search, account, and bag icons may appear only when they are intentionally styled as the approved compact utility capsule or replaced by approved demo/contact actions.

Header traits:

- Fixed or sticky at the top.
- Semi-transparent or soft-metal background.
- Rounded pill buttons with thin gray borders.
- Minimal line icons.
- Quiet UI that does not compete with hero media.

Recommended reference layout:

- Left: logo or robot mark inside a small rounded pill.
- Center: pill navigation, such as `Robot`, `Models`, `Event`, and `About`, or the approved buyer-facing equivalents.
- Right: compact icon group for search, account, and bag only when those flows are intentional.

## UI Components

- Buttons: small rounded capsules, light-blue primary accents where appropriate, uppercase only for compact labels.
- Navigation: floating pill layout, thin borders, white translucent fill, minimal icons.
- Cards: image-first, rounded corners, clean white background, generous spacing, no heavy shadows.
- Sections: clean grid, spacious composition, thin dividers, and clear hierarchy.
- Motion: soft and restrained. Hover states should be minimal.

## Homepage Structure

1. **Hero**
   - Full-width real robot, product render, or coffee-service media.
   - Concrete headline, not abstract mission language.
   - Suggested tone: "Robotic coffee, built for real service."
   - Primary CTA: "Book a Demo"
   - Secondary CTA: "Explore Artly"
   - Optional proof line: "Over 1,242,696 cups of coffee served since 2021" only if source-approved; highlight the number in light blue.

2. **Proof**
   - Show locations, partners, press, deployment facts, or operational credibility.
   - Every major claim should have proof nearby.

3. **How Artly Works**
   - Explain the system in three or four simple steps:
     - Configure the experience.
     - Deploy the robot.
     - Serve consistent drinks.
     - Monitor and support operations.

4. **The Artly System**
   - Present hardware, AI, coffee craft, and service operations as one integrated system.
   - Use split layouts with robot media and concise feature modules.

5. **Commercial Use Cases**
   - Include relevant spaces such as corporate campuses, hospitality, retail, events, universities, and transit.
   - Copy should be practical and buyer-oriented.

6. **Coffee Experience**
   - Make the experience feel warm and human.
   - Show drink quality, ordering, pickup, interaction, and consistency.

7. **Deployment and Support**
   - Explain installation, maintenance, uptime, remote monitoring, training, and scaling.
   - This section should reduce buyer anxiety.

8. **Shopify Commerce / Inquiry Flow**
   - Use clear product/service cards, demo request CTAs, solution pages, and contact flows.
   - Keep Shopify interactions familiar and easy.

## Motion

Use restrained, premium motion only:

- Slow hero video.
- Gentle scroll reveals.
- Subtle hover states.
- Simple system-flow animations.
- Reduced-motion support.

## Avoid

- Unsupported "proven" or "revolutionary" claims.
- Generic AI buzzwords.
- Overly dark sci-fi design.
- Developer-only language.
- Consumer home robot positioning.
- Excessive cards.
- Decorative orbs, blobs, and gradients.
- Copy that explains features without showing proof.

## Five-Second Test

Within five seconds, a first-time visitor should understand:

**Artly is a real robotic coffee system for commercial spaces, and it can be deployed, supported, and trusted.**
